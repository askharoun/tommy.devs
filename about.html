About Me

Welcome. My name is Thomas Askharoun, currently in my first year of pursuing a degree in Computer Engineering complemented by a minor in Cybersecurity Computational Foundations at The Pennsylvania State University. My academic journey is driven by a profound interest in Hardware, and Cybersecurity where I am dedicated to exploring and advancing my expertise in both fields.

Personal Background

My journey started in Egypt and, at the age of nine, my family and I made a big leap to the US, where I've been soaking up every bit of culture and experience since. Ever since I was a kid, I've had a thing for computers. At eleven, I wasn't just playing games—I was that kid who wanted to know how the games were made. Fast forward to today, and I'm in Computer Engineering at Penn State, with a side of Cybersecurity, because why not? I got to test my coding at Esync Technologies as a software engineering intern. It wasn't just about getting my hands dirty with code; it was about being part of a team that's out there solving real problems. When I'm not tangled up in code, you'll find me lacing up my running shoes or chilling out with a good TV series (Suits is a really good option). There's something about following a show, episode by episode, that beats a two-hour movie for me. I'm all about making technology safe and secure for everyone-I have a thing for pen testing. It's not just an academic thing; it's personal. This website? It's my playground. Built it from scratch with HTML and CSS to really get into the guts of web development.
